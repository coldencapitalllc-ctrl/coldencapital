Who We Are

Not a fund.
A family enterprise.

Colden Capital is a family-owned private enterprise based in South Carolina. We are not a registered investment advisor, broker-dealer, or financial institution of any kind.

We treat the market as a capital engine — using stock options, futures, and long-dated equity contracts to generate capital that funds real, income-producing asset acquisitions.

Our model connects two worlds: the precision of financial markets and the permanence of Main Street assets. Finance education generates the seed. The market grows it. Bank leverage amplifies it. Hard assets and franchises convert it all into durable, generational cash flow.

Every position in the market has a target — a dump truck, a trailer park, an apartment building, a franchise location — something tangible that generates income for the family for decades.

Discipline First

Every entry is rule-based and systematic. We don't chase — we wait for our criteria to align before deploying capital into the market.

Market to Main Street

Options gains and leveraged capital are redeployed into real, brick-and-mortar income assets — trucks, real estate, and franchise businesses.

Generational Thinking

Every decision is made with the next generation in mind. We build infrastructure and ownership — not just portfolios or trading accounts.

How It Works

The Flywheel

I

Literacy & Digital Income

Financial education and digital asset creation — courses, content, consulting — generate the first capital and build the discipline that drives every decision downstream.

II

Market Execution

Capital enters via stock options and futures — LEAPs, PUTs, short-term plays. Defined risk, high conviction, asymmetric upside. The account grows systematically.

III

Bank Leverage

A grown account unlocks SBLOCs and portfolio margin loans secured against account value. Capital is amplified without liquidating positions.

IV

Hard Asset Deployment

Leveraged capital acquires income-producing assets: 18-wheeler trailer rentals, dump trucks, land, trailer parks, apartments, and commercial real estate.

V

Franchise & Reinvest

Cash flow funds franchises — car washes, laundromats, food services. Surplus reinvests into Stage II, compounding the flywheel each turn.
